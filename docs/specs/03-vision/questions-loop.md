# Questions Loop

> **Role in the project:** A structured self-interrogation exercise (July 2025) used to validate the project's direction before committing to full development. Each question challenges a core assumption about purpose, value, and priority.

> **Preceded by:** [Automated Solution Concept](./automated-solution-concept.md)
>
> **Leads to:** [Product Building Research](../04-product-design/product-building-research.md)

*Last updated: 16 / 07 / 2025*

**1. Why are we doing this? Who is this for? Who benefits?**

I want to build my project with a valid idea, in a way that lets me start developing and executing directly. So it's for creators of software/SaaS/developers who want to be more productive. The maker benefits from better productivity, and the SaaS market also benefits.

**2. What specific problem are we solving? Is the customer confused or blocked?**

Solving the problem of starting development with an idea that doesn't have good product-market fit. Without having real insight into future customers. The customer may lack experience in building successful products.

**3. Is this actually useful? *(Don't mistake enthusiasm for usefulness.)*

It's useful for me because I always build projects that are poor in marketing terms.

**4. Are we adding real value? Will this help users get more out of our main product?**

We add value in the sense that we improve on what ChatGPT offers by specialising in this domain. Currently I am improving the core product.

**5. Will this change behaviour? Are we creating a real, visible impact?**

Yes, it will change everything.

**6. Is there an easier way?**

No.

**7. What could we be doing instead?**

Frontend — but the core is more important.

**8. Is it really worth it right now?**

Yes.
